## 1. Triage — the load-bearing prose passage

The argument is a close reading of one specific quoted text. Orwell's
prose passage — the six rules of clear writing — is the artifact the film
will annotate by phrase. The film does not paraphrase the rules; it

Two specific phrases in the source text are load-bearing:

- "Never use a metaphor, simile or other figure of speech which you are

- "Break any of these rules sooner than say anything outright barbarous."
  — the closing rule that undoes the prior five.

## 2. What the prose says

The essay claims political language is decayed prose, and decayed prose
both reflects and enables decayed thought. The six rules are the
prescription. The film argues from the primary source — the verse-like
cadence of Orwell's prose is itself part of the case.

## 3. The hard parts

- **The misconception** — the essay is often read as a style guide. The
  close reading shows it is a political argument that uses style as
  evidence.
- **Where it breaks** — Rule 6 (break any of these rules) destabilizes the
  other five. The film must annotate the contradiction without explaining
  it away.

## 4. Verdict

Sound — the essay's prose is its argument, and the close reading must
quote it, not summarize it.
