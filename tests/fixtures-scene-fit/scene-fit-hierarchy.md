# Survey — scene-fit-hierarchy (hermetic fixture)

Mode: ex
Subject: The Linnaean taxonomy — life's parent-child classification

This is a synthetic survey used by `docent hermetic-scene-fit` to verify the
mapper pulls the `tree` primitive when the subject's structure is parent-child
and the levels mean something.

## 0. Content boundary

The film covers the seven canonical taxonomic ranks (kingdom → phylum → class
→ order → family → genus → species) for the domain Eukarya. The cladistic
revision and the bacterial / archaeal trees are out of scope.

## 1. Triage — the load-bearing structure

The argument IS the hierarchy. Linnaeus's taxonomy is a rooted tree where
the levels encode a classification axis — kingdom is broader than phylum,
which is broader than class, and so on. Every node lives at one specific
level, and the parent-child edges carry biological meaning: a class IS a
kind of phylum.

This is not a structure with arbitrary edges; it is a taxonomy with
levels that mean something. The depth on the tree IS the classification
strength.

## 2. What it is

A seven-level rooted tree. The root is "Eukarya"; the leaves are individual
species. Each level corresponds to a real biological rank — the levels
have a name, and the depth is not decorative.

## 3. The hard parts

- **Where the levels strain** — fungi were placed in Plantae for two
  centuries before the kingdom-level distinction was redrawn. The rank
  itself is the artifact under stress.
- **The misconception** — the tree is not phylogenetic; the cladistic
  tree shows ancestry, which is a different structural claim.

## 4. Verdict

A clean parent-child taxonomy is rare in biology and Linnaeus's survives
because the levels were chosen to mean something.
